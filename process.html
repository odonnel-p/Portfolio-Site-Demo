About Patrick

“

[Sketches] relate far more to an activity or process (the conversation), rather than a physical object or artifact (the sketch). ”

Bill Buxton, Sketching User Experiences (2007)

A designer, Patrick firmly believes in a documentation of process. Sketches—on paper, digital mockups, prototypes, or even scribbles on a cocktail napkin—act as indices for the process of design. As a tool, these physical investments define a shared language among all those participating in the process. As an experience, these sketches embody a vagueness that permits participants to ask questions. Sketches tend to highlight crucial decisions in the life of projects as well as allow reflection on the creative process.

Process Journal

This Process Journal began as a catalogue of various graduate school assignments (readings, practices, ideation, research) but has continued on as a form of explanation that sometimes eludes the abstracted presentation of most creative portfolios. With each project suitable for a specific client and a specific user in a specific context, a journal entry can better explain the link between the design choices and ultimate goals of the project.
